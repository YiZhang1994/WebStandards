What are web standards?

"Web standards are the formal, non-proprietary standards and other technical specifications that define and describe aspects of the World Wide Web" (Wikipedia, 2016). Web standards are designed to ensure validity of documents published online, provide incompatibility solution, and thus to present the web pages or sites in a more user-friendly way (WasP, 1998).

Web standards contain a set of standards and specifications, which directly or indirectly affect web development. "Considerations include the interoperability, accessibility and usability of web pages and web sites" (Wikipedia, 2016).

Broader sense composition

  • Recommendations published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)
  • "Living Standards" made by the Web Hypertext Application Technology Working Group (WHATWG)
  • Request for Comments (RFC) documents published by the Internet Engineering Task Force
  • Standards published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
  • Standards published by Ecma International (formerly ECMA)
  • The Unicode Standard and various Unicode Technical Reports (UTRs) published by the Unicode Consortium
  • Name and number registries maintained by the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA)

(Source: Wikipedia)
